Appendix A: Different node inertia coefficients and SCR calculation process

For each scenario, the multi-site short-circuit ratio (MRSCR) for renewable energy stations is calculated by Equation(A1)

$$ MRSCR_{t}=\left|\frac{\dot{U}_{i}^{*}\dot{U}_{Nt}}{\dot{Z}_{\text{eqt}}}\right|/\left|\dot{S}_{\text{RE}}+\sum_{j=1}^{n}\dot{\Pi}_{ij}\,\dot{S}_{\text{REj}}\right|\qquad(A1)$$

where $\dot{U}_{i}$ is the actual operating voltage at the $i$-th renewable bus, and $\dot{U}_{Nt}$ denotes the rated voltage at the same bus. $\dot{Z}_{eqt}$ is the diagonal element of the equivalent impedance matrix, representing the self-impedance at the point of common coupling. $\dot{S}_{REi}$ refers to the complex apparent power injected by the renewable resource at bus $i$. The coupling coefficient between $i$-th and $j$-th buses is defined as:

$$ \dot{\Pi}_{ij}=\frac{\dot{Z}_{eqt}\dot{U}_{i}^{*}}{\dot{Z}_{eqt}\dot{U}_{j}^{*}\dot{Z}_{eqt}} $$

The spatial variation of system inertia is reflected in the fact that, following a disturbance in the power system, different nodes exhibit significantly different frequency responses. To characterize this phenomenon, a system nodal inertia matrix is defined, representing the observed inertia at various nodes when a specific node experiences a disturbance. The matrix is expressed by Equation(A2):

$$ H=\begin{bmatrix}H_{t1}&H_{t2}&\cdots&H_{tN}\end{bmatrix}^{T}\qquad(A2) $$

Where $H_{ij}$ denotes the system inertia observed at node $j$ when a disturbance is applied at node $i$. Specifically, $H_{ii}$ represents the self-inertia coefficient of node $i$, while $H_{ij}$ (for $i \neq j$) quantifies the mutual inertia observed at node $j$ due to a disturbance at node $i$. These coefficients are calculated by Equations(A3)-(A4):

$$ H_{it}=-\Delta P_{e-l}\,/(2 df_{l}/dt)\qquad(A3) $$ $$ H_{ij}=-\Delta P_{e-l}\,/(2 df_{j}/dt)\qquad(A4) $$

Where $\Delta P_{e-l}$ denotes the change in total system active power caused by a disturbance at node $i$, and $f_{i}$, $f_{j}$ represent the frequency measured at $i$-th and $j$-th nodes, respectively. These expressions quantitatively capture the coupling between frequency dynamics and the spatial distribution of system inertia across different nodes.

For each operational scenario, the maximum value of MRSCR and the mutual inertia coefficient are identified for each node. The normalized node inertia coefficient $H_i$ and the normalized short-circuit ratio $SCR_i$ are then computed for each node individually.
